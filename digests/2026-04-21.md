**Claude Code Daily** — 2026-04-21

Today also brings meaningful quality-of-life improvements in v2.1.116, including a 67% faster `/resume` for heavy users with large sessions.

**Big News**
- **[Amazon invests $25B in Anthropic](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/20/amazon-invest-up-to-25-billion-in-anthropic-part-of-ai-infrastructure.html)** — $5B immediately at a $380B valuation, plus up to $20B more tied to commercial milestones. In return, Anthropic commits $100B to AWS over 10 years and will run on Amazon Trainium chips. Comes two months after Amazon's $50B OpenAI deal — a clear sign the compute wars are accelerating.
- **[Claude Sonnet 4 & Opus 4 retiring June 15](https://platform.claude.com/docs/en/about-claude/model-deprecations)** — The original `claude-sonnet-4-20250514` and `claude-opus-4-20250514` API IDs will stop working on June 15. Current 4.6 and 4.7 variants are *not* affected. Migrate to `claude-sonnet-4-6` or `claude-opus-4-7` now to avoid breakage.
- **v2.1.116 ships** — `/resume` is now 67% faster on sessions over 40MB (a real difference for long-running projects). MCP startup is faster when you have multiple stdio servers configured, and the thinking spinner now shows progress inline ("still thinking", "almost done").

**Power User Tips**
- **`/autofix-pr` is a one-liner for set-and-forget PRs** — Run it on your branch and Claude watches CI and review comments, pushing fixes until the PR is green, without you staying in the loop. Launched Week 15 but still underused.
- **`/cost` now shows a per-model + cache-hit breakdown** — If you're on a subscription and wondering where your quota went, run `/cost` mid-session. Cache hits are listed separately so you can see what you're actually paying compute for.
- **`Ctrl+O` in fullscreen mode** — Collapses the view to just your last prompt, a one-line tool summary with diffstats, and Claude's final response. Cuts noise when reviewing output on a long agentic run.
- **`/team-onboarding` for repo handoffs** — Run it in a project you know well and hand the output to a new teammate. It packages your Claude Code setup — skills, hooks, CLAUDE.md conventions — into a replayable guide.
